Fueling your skeleton

 

Your bones act as your body's 'calcium bank' 99% of your body's calcium is stored in your bones. If your body does not receive enough calcium in what you eat and drink to perform its other functions, such as helping your muscles and heart to function, the body withdraws calcium from the 'bank' and leaves your bones weak and brittle. That means you will be more likely to break or fracture a bone than a person who gets lots of calcium!

When your bones are growing and repairing, they demand calcium! The best way to have strong bones is to eat a diet rich in calcium.

You also need vitamin D to help your body absorb calcium you get from your diet. We absorb most of our vitamin D through sunlight.
