Broken bones

Your bones are strong, but even strong things can break! Have you ever broken a bone? The intense pain you get isn't actually from the bone itself breaking; it's from the damaged caused to the periosteum that covers your bones. This is where all the nerves are!

There are different types of bone breaks, but the most common are a thin hairline fracture and a complete break where the bone snaps in two! A complete break takes longer to heal than a hairline fracture because more of the bone needs to be replaced.

When you fracture a bone, new bone is created around the break, and is slowly remodelled to form the shape of your original bone.
