The structure of sleep

There are two kinds of sleep: Rapid Eye Movement (REM) sleep and Non- REM sleep.

During REM sleep, our brainwaves look very similar to being awake and this is your dreaming sleep. When we are in this kind of sleep, all of our muscles relax except our eyes, which move rapidly from side to side. Kind of like you're watching a fast tennis match with your eyes closed!

Non-REM sleep is made up of three stages. The first stage, called N1, is a very light sleep and occurs when we are falling asleep. The second stage, called N2, is a heavier sleep and makes up most of our sleep time. The deepest sleep we have is called N3, also known as slow wave sleep because our brain waves slow right down. About five or six times a night, you will go from N1, quickly move to N2, then on to N3 before getting some REM sleep. At the end of each cycle, you will wake up, roll over and go back to sleep. These awakenings are usually so short that you won't remember them in the morning!
