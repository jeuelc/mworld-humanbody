What are they made of?

Your bones are made of many compounds including calcium, just like your teeth. This compound is what makes your bones hard! But your bones also contain collagen, which is bendy like rope! Together these provide the best framework for your bones to be strong AND flexible!

Each of your bones are made of two layers. The first is a hard outer shell, which is called compact bone. Touch your arm bone - that's the hard part! Inside is the second spongy layer. But it's not soft like a sponge - it has a lot of holes in it like a honeycomb! This allows your bones to be strong but light.

All of your bones are wrapped in a material called the periosteum. It contains lots of nerves, which makes it very sensitive. Have you ever been kicked in the shins? The pain you feel is from hurting the nerves in your periosteum.
