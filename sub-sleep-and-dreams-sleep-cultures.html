Sleep across cultures

 
click here

There are many cultural differences in the way people sleep. In many countries, the most common sleeping pattern is to have one long sleep during the night and be awake all day. However, this is not the case in every country.

In some parts of South America and Southern Europe, people often sleep or rest for two or more hours during the day and stay up late into the night. This daytime rest is known as a siesta.

In many Asian countries, people have a short sleep at night time, but then take a few very short naps during the day. In Japan, these short naps are called inemuri. Children are allowed to have a nap during class and the teachers let them sleep.
