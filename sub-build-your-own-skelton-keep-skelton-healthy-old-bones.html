Old bones

 

When you age, sometimes your bones can lose their strength and density. This process is called osteoporosis and it literally means 'bones with holes'. It occurs when bones lose calcium more quickly than the body replaces it. Bones become weak and brittle, so they tend to fracture more easily.

Osteoporosis most commonly affects elderly people, and you can prevent osteoporosis by taking care of your bones in your teenage years.

People with osteoporosis can reduce their bone loss with a calcium-rich diet (or calcium tablets). They can also help maintain their bone density by doing moderate weight-bearing exercise, such as walking and weight programs. Other exercises such as Yoga and Pilates can improve their balance and reduce their risk of falls.

There are other diseases that affect your bones as you get older such as arthritis and rickets. Therefore it is really important to look after your bones throughout your life.
