Making blood!

The inside of your bones create red and white blood cells. Red blood cells carry oxygen to your entire body and white blood cells help you fight infection.

In babies and young children, blood cells are made in the innermost part of the bone called the bone marrow. As we grow older, only some bones keep producing blood cells.

You might have heard about the illness called leukaemia, which is cancer of a person's white blood cells. This cancer means the person doesn't have enough healthy white blood cells to fight infection. So these people get sick very easily. If the person can get some bone marrow from another person's body, it will help them replace their healthy white blood cells

So your skeleton might have the potential to save someone's life!
