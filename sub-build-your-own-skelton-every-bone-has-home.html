Every bone has a home

All of your bones are arranged in a specific manner. Most are structured in a symmetrical fashion. That is, many of your bones are matched on both sides of your body.

Your body connects only certain bones to one another via joints, muscles and ligaments. Joints help move your bones so you can perform many functions. For example the shoulder girdle connects your arms to your body.

Have a look at a skeleton, or check out a model skeleton, to see what your bones look like arranged together! Can you tell which bones are long bones? Can you tell which bones are short, which are irregular and which are flat and sesamoid?
